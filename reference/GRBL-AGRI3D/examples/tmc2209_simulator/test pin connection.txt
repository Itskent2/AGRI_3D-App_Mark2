=========================================
GRBL-AGRI3D TMC2209 SIMULATOR TEST WIRING
=========================================

To test the GRBL-AGRI3D master firmware against the TMC2209 Simulator, 
you need two Arduino Nanos.

[ NANO 1: THE MASTER (GRBL-AGRI3D) ]
This Nano runs the main `testgrbl` or `grblUpload` firmware.
It generates the Step/Dir pulses and sends TMC UART commands.

[ NANO 2: THE SIMULATOR (tmc2209_simulator) ]
This Nano runs the simulator sketch. It reads the pulses and simulates 
the TMC2209 driver's diagnostics and phase states.


--- WIRING CONNECTIONS ---

1. GROUND (CRITICAL)
   Master GND  <----->  Simulator GND

2. STEP & DIR
   Connect the specific axis you want to test from the Master.
   For example, testing the X-Axis on the Master:
   Master D2 (X-Step)  <----->  Simulator D2 (STEP)
   Master D5 (X-Dir)   <----->  Simulator D3 (DIR)

3. UART SERIAL
   Connect the Master's hardware/software UART pins to the Simulator.
   If the Master is using standard serial for TMC communication:
   Master D12 (TX)  <----->  Simulator D10 (RX)
   Master D8  (RX)  <----->  Simulator D11 (TX)
   
   *Note: If GRBL-AGRI3D uses a specific single-wire PDN_UART configuration 
   on a single pin, connect that single pin to BOTH D10 and D11 on the Simulator 
   with a 1k resistor on the TX line (or directly if just testing logic).*

4. EDGE CASE SELECTOR (ON SIMULATOR NANO ONLY)
   Use jumper wires connected to the Simulator's Ground (GND) to 
   pull these pins LOW and trigger errors:
   - D4 (Bit 1)
   - D5 (Bit 0)
   
   States:
   [D4 OPEN, D5 OPEN] -> Normal Operation
   [D4 OPEN, D5 GND ] -> Overtemperature Error (OT)
   [D4 GND,  D5 OPEN] -> Overtemperature Warning (OTPW)
   [D4 GND,  D5 GND ] -> Short to Ground (S2G)


--- HOW TO TEST ---
1. Power up both Nanos via USB.
2. Open two instances of the Arduino IDE or a serial terminal program.
3. Connect one terminal to the Master (baud rate 115200) to send G-code (e.g., $X, G1 X10 F500).
4. Connect the second terminal to the Simulator (baud rate 115200) to view the phase outputs and see the simulated UART responses to GRBL.
5. Trigger edge cases by physically connecting Simulator D4/D5 to GND and watch the Master trigger alarms/feedholds in its terminal.
